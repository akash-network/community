## Burn Mint Equilibrium (BME) Incentivized Testnet

### Launch Overview

* BME incentivized testnet officially began **February 17** and runs through **March 3, 2026**.
* Instructions, overview, and submission forms have been published and were shared again during the call.
* Reward pool: **$10,000** for participants who complete tasks correctly and meet eligibility requirements.

### Background & Preparation

* BME has undergone extensive community discussion since last year and was shaped through feedback on Akash Enhancement Proposals.
* Core engineering team internally tested BME functionality before involving Insiders.
* Insider testers successfully identified additional issues, which core team fixed before public release.

### Public Testnet Status

* Expected heavy validator and community participation.

## Oracle Integration & Follow-Up Testing

### Current Phase

* The current incentivized testnet uses **manual price feeds**.

### Next Phase

* A follow-up internal testnet includes **Pyth oracle contracts**, which are already deployed and functioning successfully.
* There may be a **smaller public oracle testnet** to complement the main BME test phase.
* Oracle integration is part of a larger sequence of upcoming network upgrades.

### Supporting External Developer Teams

* George asked about helping a team building a food delivery/consumer app migrate from AWS to Akash.
* Tyler clarified:

  * SIG Chain is not the best place for credit or onboarding programs.
  * Recommended starting with showing how Akash deployments work and identifying compute requirements.

### Testnet Technical Requirements

* George asked if the BME testnet requires deep coding knowledge.
* Tyler and Scott clarified:

  * **No coding required**, but **command-line and Linux/MacOS familiarity** is needed.
  * Must use **Akash CLI**, not Console.
  * The test is fully CLI-driven and assumes basic system navigation skills.
