## Meeting Notes

### Opening Remarks & Governance Update

  * Governance proposals.
  * Token economics.
  * Economic strategy.

### AKT Inflation Reduction Discussion

* Rodri R raised concerns regarding the recently approved inflation adjustment proposal.
* Rodri observed that staking APR had fallen significantly following implementation.
* Concern was expressed that lower staking yields could reduce incentives for token holders to continue staking AKT.
* Rodri noted that APR appeared substantially lower than anticipated.
* Questions were raised regarding:

  * Long-term staking attractiveness.
  * Token holder incentives.
  * Impact on network participation.
  * Future value proposition for AKT holders.

### Inflation Schedule Alignment

* Cheng Wang acknowledged the concerns and provided context behind the proposal.
* Cheng explained that Akash had drifted above its originally published inflation schedule.
* Current token emissions exceeded projected levels established during the network's earlier economic planning.
* The inflation proposal was designed to bring emissions back into alignment with long-term expectations.
* Cheng emphasized that maintaining credibility around published economic schedules is important for the network.
* Returning to the intended emissions path was described as a necessary correction rather than a strategic shift.

### Supply Cap Considerations

* Cheng discussed the importance of managing emissions carefully because AKT remains a supply-capped asset.
* Excessive inflation could create long-term challenges for the network.
* Future network growth initiatives will require available token supply for:

  * Ecosystem incentives.
  * Community initiatives.
  * Strategic growth programs.
  * Future development efforts.
* Continuing elevated emissions indefinitely was described as unsustainable.
* Cheng noted that inflation must eventually be reduced to preserve long-term economic flexibility.

### Historical Staking Rewards Perspective

* Cheng pointed out that stakers had benefited from higher-than-planned inflation for an extended period.
* The network delayed inflation adjustments longer than originally intended.
* As a result:

  * Stakers received above-target rewards.
  * Emissions exceeded planned schedules.
  * Supply growth accelerated beyond projections.
* The current proposal serves as a correction mechanism rather than introducing a new economic philosophy.
* Current emission rates are expected to gradually bring the network back toward its intended inflation trajectory over approximately the next year.

### Inflation, Supply, and Token Value

* Cheng discussed the relationship between inflation and token economics.
* Lower emissions reduce the amount of new supply entering circulation.
* Reduced supply growth may contribute positively to long-term token economics.
* Cheng emphasized that inflation should not be viewed in isolation.
* Long-term token value depends on:

  * Product adoption.
  * Ecosystem growth.
  * Partnerships.
  * Revenue generation.
  * Demand for network services.
* Continued execution and growth were highlighted as more important drivers of value than staking yields alone.

### Staking Rewards vs Speculative Assets

* Rodri questioned whether low staking rewards remain attractive compared to stablecoin-based yield opportunities.
* Cheng responded by distinguishing between speculative assets and stable assets.
* He argued that investors purchasing volatile assets typically do so because they expect long-term appreciation.
* Staking rewards should be viewed as an additional benefit rather than the primary investment thesis.
* Cheng noted that:

  * Investors seeking predictable yield may prefer stablecoins.
  * Investors holding AKT are generally participating because they believe in future network growth.
* The discussion emphasized that staking rewards alone should not define the attractiveness of a growth-oriented network asset.

### Additional Perspective on APR Reduction

* Isaac Grooms provided additional commentary regarding staking incentives.
* Isaac stated that excessively high APRs can create concerns around dilution.
* Lower inflation rates may improve overall token attractiveness by reducing supply expansion.
* Isaac expressed support for the proposal and agreed that the adjustment strengthens long-term network economics.
* While acknowledging that reduced rewards may be disappointing for stakers, he argued that the broader economic benefits outweigh the short-term reduction in yield.

### Future Migration and Tokenomics Discussion

* Discussion shifted toward the long-term future of AKT tokenomics.
* Rodri raised questions about how token economics may evolve if Akash migrates away from its current architecture.
* Topics discussed included:

  * Staking mechanisms.
  * Supply management.
  * Inflation models.
  * Future token utility.
* Cheng emphasized that future tokenomic structures remain under active exploration.
* No final decisions have been made regarding:

  * Staking models.
  * Inflation mechanisms.
  * Supply management frameworks.
  * Governance incentives.

### Supply Cap and Future Expansion Scenarios

* Cheng discussed circumstances under which increasing supply could potentially become appropriate.
* One example involved significant growth in network economic activity.
* If Akash eventually facilitates extremely large transaction volumes and economic throughput, additional supply mechanisms may become necessary.
* Cheng explained that an overly constrained monetary supply could eventually limit network efficiency.
* Economic growth and utility requirements must be considered alongside scarcity.
* Any future supply adjustments would require substantial analysis and strong justification.
* Cheng stressed that no such plans currently exist.
* The topic remains purely exploratory and theoretical at this stage.

### Economic Redesign Opportunities

* Cheng described the potential migration period as an opportunity to rethink network economics from first principles.
* The team is actively brainstorming potential future models.
* Areas under consideration include:

  * Incentive alignment.
  * Staking structures.
  * Utility mechanisms.
  * Network growth incentives.
  * Long-term sustainability.
* Cheng encouraged community participation in the design process.
* Community members were invited to contribute ideas and proposals.
* Emphasis was placed on building a stronger system rather than focusing solely on potential losses or changes.
