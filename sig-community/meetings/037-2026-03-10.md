### Opening Remarks & Community Direction

### Akash Club — Event Restructuring

* Weekly event cadence has led to event fatigue and declining participation consistency
* Decision made to reduce frequency and prioritize higher-value events
* Shift toward more practical and utility-driven sessions rather than maintaining volume
* Plan to gather community input via Discord to identify relevant topics and interests
* Workshops and tutorials identified as high-demand formats for learning and engagement
* Feature-related sessions such as roadmap discussions and product releases consistently attract higher turnout
* Casual events such as networking sessions and game nights showed lower engagement
* Casual events will continue but at reduced frequency
* Focus on reallocating effort toward activities that deliver measurable value to the community

### Community Contributions & Guides

* Strong community participation observed in creating guides and technical documentation
* Recognition given to contributors including Rodri and Sandeep for active contributions
* Focus on simplifying onboarding for new users through accessible guides
* Plan to centralize all guides into a dedicated section on the Akash website
* Emphasis on maintaining consistent quality standards across all documentation
* Goal to ensure long-term accessibility and continuous updates of guides

### Deployment Templates & Usability

* Identified gap between available templates and user understanding of how to use them
* Strategy to pair every template with a corresponding guide for usability
* Aim to create complete deployment packages combining templates and instructions
* Objective to increase adoption by improving ease of use
* Focus on ensuring that contributions have real impact and are actively utilized

### Akash Agents & AI Ecosystem

* Introduction of Akash agents platform and ongoing development of agent frameworks
* Preview shared via agents.akash.network for deploying agents such as OpenClaw and Hermes
* Recognition that multiple agent frameworks require community-driven documentation
* Encouragement for contributors to create guides explaining usage of different frameworks
* Upcoming integration of Qwen models into Akash console
* Alignment between community contributions and Akash ML roadmap
* Strong focus on leveraging the growing AI agent ecosystem

### Community Bounty Program Redesign

* Previous bounty board approach deemed inefficient and overly complex
* Transition to a contributor-driven bounty model
* Contributors encouraged to propose ideas based on personal interest and use cases
* Bounties issued dynamically based on approved ideas
* Increased collaboration between Joe Deng and contributors to ensure high-quality output
* Focus on meaningful contributions rather than task assignment
* Potential future reintroduction of structured bounty board
* Example highlighted where contributor-led idea resulted in successful deployment and guide pairing

### Hackathon Strategy & Event Direction

* Hackathons shifting from community-led to core team-managed initiatives
* Events now primarily organized by the events team under Amanda
* Increased focus on in-person hackathons targeting high-value participants
* Emphasis on measurable outcomes such as user onboarding and platform usage
* Budget allocation tied to performance and impact
* Community-led hackathons remain possible but require stronger validation
* Strategic focus on AI developers and startups as primary participants
