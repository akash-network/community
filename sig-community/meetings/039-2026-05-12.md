## Meeting Notes

### Opening Remarks & Technical Discussion

* Joe Deng welcomed attendees and officially opened the Community SIG meeting.
* Prior to the formal presentation, participants discussed deployment infrastructure and scaling requirements.
* Sandeep raised concerns around the lack of community-maintained load balancing solutions for Akash deployments.
* Discussion focused on the need for custom NGINX or Traefik Docker images to simplify workload scaling.
* Sandeep explained that users currently need to create their own scripts and deployment logic when implementing load balancing.
* Joe Deng acknowledged the value of the idea and requested a formal specification for review.
* Contributor agreement distribution was briefly discussed.
* Sandeep confirmed receipt of his contributor agreement.

### Community Application Abstraction Strategy

* Joe Deng discussed efforts to simplify onboarding for non-technical users.
* Current workflows require:

  * Akash ML credentials.
  * Console API credentials.
  * Manual deployment management.
* The long-term goal is to abstract infrastructure complexity from end users.
* Future onboarding flow is expected to include:

  * Email registration.
  * User login.
  * Payment setup.
  * One-click deployment experiences.
* Joe Deng emphasized that all future community applications will follow this abstraction model.
* Objective is to onboard users who have no prior knowledge of Akash infrastructure.
* Community applications are intended to:

  * Demonstrate real-world Akash use cases.
  * Increase inference demand.
  * Drive adoption of Akash-hosted services.

### Akash Scribe Development Progress

* Joe Deng shared updates on Akash Scribe development.
* The project was previously known as Akash Echo before reverting to the Scribe branding.
* Working desktop applications now exist for:

  * Windows.
  * Linux.
* Akash Scribe is designed as an AI-powered transcription and dictation platform.
* Users can:

  * Activate recording with a hotkey.
  * Speak naturally.
  * Convert speech into text.
  * Automatically insert text into active applications.
* Joe Deng shared positive experiences using similar commercial dictation products.
* He emphasized the productivity advantages of speech-first workflows.
* Community testing is expected to begin within the following week.
* Public launch preparations are underway.
* Rodri R announced additional functionality including:

  * Spell checking.
  * Grammar correction.
  * AI-assisted text refinement using Llama-based models.
* Rodri continues work on abstracting infrastructure requirements from end users.
* Plans are being explored for a free usage tier for community members.
